Lasers or Dermapen for Under the Eye Wrinkles?
Fraxel, Ultherafor under eye wrinkles
Dec 20, 2012
I’ve seen great results using a series of fraxel treatments for under eye wrinkles. Ulthera also tends to soften these lines by encouraging the production of collagen. Once either or both of these treatments are done, you can consider a little bit of filler or botox to soften any remaining lines.

Looking for an Experienced Doctor in NJ/NYC Area That Does Botox Jaw Reduction?
Botox Jaw Reduction Very Effective
Dec 20, 2012
I’ve done a number of cases in which I inject botox into the jawline to taper it and make the jaw a bit more defined. I actually don’t think the technique is very difficult, but you have to make sure the patient’s anatomy is conducive to benefiting from the procedure. Then it’s a matter of finding the right dose for a patient. I usually start with conservative doses and see how someone responds. You can always add more at a follow up visit, but you can’t take botox away.
Forehead Wrinkles?
Best to combine glabella and forehead botox
Dec 20, 2012
If you treat the forehead with botox without treating the glabella, you run the risk of the patient feeling as though their forehead is “heavy.” It’s a neanderthal sensation and patients hate it. I have treated a number of patients with SIGNIFICANTLY higher doses in the forehead than what you’re worried about, without a ptosis/eyelid droop. It all depends on technique and finding a dose that’s best for the patient.
Best Laser for Lip Lines?
fraxel then belotero and botox
Dec 20, 2012
I would recommend doing a series of fraxel treatments for the lip lines (minimal downtime after each treatment) followed by Belotero injections and a drop of botox. I would do the laser first, because you might need less filler once the laser is done.
