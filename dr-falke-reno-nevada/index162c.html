Should get extraction and implant or root canal with build-up, posts, and zirconium crown on tooth #19
Root canal and crown VS implant
Oct 19, 2018
The general rule of thumb for me and my patients is if you can save the tooth and are able to get at least a few years out of it, that’s the route I would suggest. If saving the tooth is not an option or it is likely to fail very soon after restoration, we talk about extraction and implant placement. Implants are the best option only if a tooth has a poor prognosis or cannot be saved.

I have implants on my front teeth. They have chipped and another piece came off. Is this normal?
Chip on implant
Oct 17, 2018
It sounds like the implant supported crown has chipped. I would recommend calling your general dentist who made the crowns for you and scheduling a follow up with them, they may be able to do a repair on the crown, or they may recommend a new crown is made. Crowns don’t last forever so it is possible for it to chip, just like a natural tooth can chip or fracture.

Do I need a healing abutment before crown placement after implant?
Healing abutment before crown
Oct 8, 2018
The oral surgeon who placed the implant is usually the one who will uncover the implant and place the healing abutment. The healing abutment is in place for 1-2 weeks to allow the gum tissue to heal so you can get the best fitting abutment and crown from your general dentist. I understand the desire to get the crown done ASAP, those flippers are no fun, but it is better to wait 2 extra weeks and make sure it’s done right.
Best of luck!
