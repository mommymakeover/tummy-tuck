Statement: Dr. Kazlouskaya is a highly experienced dermatologist and dermatopathologist who holds dual board certifications. She specializes in diagnosing and treating a wide range of skin conditions in both adults and children. Her expertise covers various areas, including hair loss (alopecia), acne, acne scar treatment, skin cancer prevention, skin surgery, and cosmetic dermatology.

Dr. Kazlouskaya earned her MD and PhD degrees from Vitebsk State Medical University in Belarus. She completed her dermatology residency at the same institution and later went on to become an Associate Professor in dermatology, chairing the dermatovenereology department at Gomel State Medical University. Her commitment to advancing her knowledge and skills led her to pursue extensive postgraduate training in Europe, specializing in dermatopathology in Germany and gaining expertise in photodynamic therapy and laser surgery in Italy.

Upon relocating to the United States, Dr. Kazlouskaya completed a second dermatology residency at SUNY Downstate Medical Center, where she was recognized with the Best Resident Award during her third year of training. Additionally, she completed a fellowship in dermatopathology at the University of Pittsburgh.

Dr. Kazlouskaya’s contributions to the field of dermatology are substantial. She serves as a member of the Editorial Board for the Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology and actively participates in reviewing articles for various scientific dermatological journals.

Recognized for her dedication and achievements, Dr. Kazlouskaya has received prestigious awards, including the Women’s Society of Dermatology’s Career and Community Advancement Award, the Richard B. Stoughton Memorial Fellowship Award, and the Orlando Dermatology Aesthetic & Clinical Conference Social Media Award. Her commitment to advancing dermatology extends beyond accolades; she also received the AAD Resident International Grant and dedicated a month to volunteering in Botswana, where she educated local dermatologists and provided care to patients with tropical dermatological conditions.

Do I have dry skin and how to I get rid of the two spots circled in blue?
Skin care likely will not help
Aug 21, 2023
The areas you circled are atrophic scars. Unfortunately, skincare will not completely eliminate them. Creams with retinoids (specifically tazarotene) have some data to suggest they can improve the appearance of scars, but I would not rely on them too much. There is a range of cosmetic options available for improving atrophic scars, and they are most effective when used in combination (TCA cross peel, microneedling, and lasers). Please let us know if we can be of assistance.
