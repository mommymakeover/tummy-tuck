Seroma treatment? What are my options?
Seroma treatment
May 31, 2022
Seroma development after tummy tuck is very common. I agree with your doctor that compression, aspiration, and surgical removal are good options. In my experience, compression alone generally is not enough to resolve the seroma unless the body also absorbs it itself. My recommendation is to try to find someone who would be willing to aspirate this for you to remove it. It may take several times but that may avoid additional surgery

Why does my tummy tuck scar look like this 8 mo post-op? How do I fix it?
Scar revision
Jan 13, 2022
It looks as though your incision has widened somewhat, probably because of the tension on the wound which is common in doing this procedure. At eight months, you are still in the healing process and this can take upwards of two years to go to completion. By then the appearance may be improved. If not, revision surgery to a size the scar and close the one with less tension may be an option to get you a thinner and more favorable scar.

Periareolar or inframammary incision for light pink nipples?
periareolar incision
Jan 8, 2022
The plastic surgery literature would suggest that a periareolar incision has a slightly higher risk of capsular contracture, but I use a periareolar incision almost exclusively (more than 98% of the time) and have had only one patient in 28 years have an unsightly scar. It heals very well, blends well with the surrounding tissue and is easy to hide. Of course there are no guarantees, but based on your pictures that's what I would do.
