Doctors

Shelin Tkatch, MD, FRCSC

Name: Shelin Tkatch, MD, FRCSC
Statement: Dr. Shelin Tkatch has been a board-certified gynecologic surgeon in Surrey, British Columbia for more than two decades. At her practice, MD Wellness Solutions, she combines her expertise in gynecology with a diploma in dermatology to serve her patients’ needs, ranging from sexual wellness issues to nonsurgical facial rejuvenation, with warmth and empathy.

With refined technique and an eye for facial balance and symmetry, Dr. Tkatch specializes in cosmetic treatments, including dermal fillers like Juvéderm and Restylane, other injectables like Botox, and Belkyra, to treat small pockets of excess fat. A dedicated researcher of anti-aging aesthetic medicine, she offers her patients the latest result-driven treatments for nonsurgical rejuvenation.

Dr. Tkatch earned her medical degree from the University of British Columbia in Vancouver. She went on to complete an obstetrics and gynecology residency at the University of Alberta in Edmonton, and then earned a diploma in practical dermatology from Cardiff University in Wales.

In addition to her private practice, Dr. Tkatch is an active staff member in the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at Peace Arch Hospital in White Rock, B.C. She is also a clinical instructor in the faculty of medicine at the University of British Columbia.

Dr. Tkatch is a member of the Canadian Association of Aesthetic Medicine (CAAM), the College of Physicians and Surgeons of British Columbia, the Dermatology Continuing Professional Development Society, the American College of Obstetricians & Gynecologists, and the American Association of Gynecologists Laparoscopists. She is board certified by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada.

Preparing your skin for C02 laser resurfacing

Work with a provider experienced in C02 laser resurfacing to prepare your skin for your procedure. They will ensure your procedure is scheduled only after proper preparation and evaluate you for any skin conditions that may need to be treated prior to laser treatment. Best, Dr. Tkatch
Is it safe to wax after IPL sessions?
Safe to wax after IPL?
Nov 26, 2021
Yes, you are safe to wax until you feel safe enough to access your aesthetic provider for IPL or laser hair removal sessions. You will need to stop waxing however, before you resume your IPL treatments as they target the hair follicle and waxing will effect your results. Best, Dr. Tkatch
Does weight gain effect facial fillers?
Weight gain after Fillers
Nov 26, 2021
Fillers placed in April 2019 will generally have dissolved within 12-18 months, so long gone. If you are experiencing water retention (puffiness), speak to your family doctor to ensure your thyroid is functioning well and you have no metabolic issues. Your family doctor can also help refer you to a nutritionist and/or discuss prescription medications to help you lose weight. Best, Dr. Tkatch

Filler for forehead dents
Feb 13, 2020
It's hard to tell from the photo, so an in person evaluation with a very skilled filler provider in your area is your best bet.
